Key insight: UVA holds the 2nd-highest resident tuition ($51,034) of the six public schools here — only Pittsburgh ($62,416) charges more. Yet UVA's nominal growth of just +1.07%/yr over 9 years (AY 2014-15 through AY 2022-23) is the lowest of any public school in the panel; in real (inflation-adjusted) terms, UVA tuition actually declined −1.84%/yr, the best outcome for students among all public programs. Despite an endowment ($14.3B) comparable to elite private schools, UVA is not converting its wealth into aggressive tuition increases. Under proposed federal graduate PLUS loan caps, however, UVA students at $51K still face exposure — the nominal price tag is what triggers debt thresholds, regardless of real-dollar trends.
