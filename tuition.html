Policy Impact Analysis

Policy · OBBB · Medical Students
One Big Beautiful Bill — Impact on Medical School Financing

Quantifies the total borrowing cost impact of the One Big Beautiful Bill (H.R. 1) on medical students at 24 U.S. MD programs. The OBBB eliminates Graduate PLUS loans and caps professional student federal borrowing at $50,000/year ($200K aggregate). Students exceeding these caps must finance remaining costs with higher-rate private loans. Analysis models 4-year in-school interest accrual plus a 10-year standard repayment to compare total cost-with-interest under current law versus OBBB.

Charts include: annual federal funding gap per school, total projected cost comparison (current law vs. OBBB), monthly payment comparison, and a sector × tercile breakdown of mean additional cost.

Data & Methodology

Sources & Methods

Tuition (Medical)
AAMC Tuition & Student Fees Reports — extracted directly from official Excel files via custom parser. All 25 MD programs, AY 2013–14 through AY 2022–23. Resident/in-state rates for public schools.
Endowment Data
NACUBO-Commonfund Study of Endowments (FY2022 anchor). Index-adjusted using NACUBO-reported annual returns to produce school-level estimates for FY2014–FY2023.
NIH Funding Tiers
Blue Ridge Institute for Medical Research (BRIMR) annual rankings. High >$400M/yr, Mid $150–400M/yr, Low <$150M/yr (FY2018–2022 average).
CPI Adjustment
BLS CPI-U national annual rates. Real tuition growth = nominal annual pct change minus CPI-U.
OBBB Loan Model
$50K/yr annual cap, $200K aggregate (professional students). Prior undergrad debt: $37K (AAMC median). Federal rate 7.94%; Grad PLUS 8.94% (current); private gap 11%. 4-yr in-school accrual + 10-yr standard repayment.
Regression
OLS: mean annual tuition growth rate (9-yr) on log(endowment). Separate estimates for public and private sub-sectors. β and p-values reported in each visualization.
